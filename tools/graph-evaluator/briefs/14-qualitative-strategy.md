We're a 30-person design agency that's always done bespoke client work. Several competitors have started offering productised design subscriptions — fixed monthly fee for unlimited design requests. We're debating whether to launch our own subscription offering, which could cannibalise our £80-per-hour bespoke work but would provide recurring revenue and reduce the feast-or-famine cycle. Our current utilisation rate is about 65% and we lose roughly a third of prospects on price before they see our work quality. The creative director is concerned that subscription work will lower our brand positioning.
